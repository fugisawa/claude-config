## Quick map by domain
- **Hyperscalers / capex / cloud** → SEC EDGAR (10-K/10-Q, earnings calls), Synergy/Canalys/Statista for share, Stratechery, SemiAnalysis, The Information.
- **Social media / attention** → company filings + transparency reports, DSA Transparency Database, EU/regulator texts, Platformer, 404 Media, Rest of World, peer-reviewed recommender studies.
- **Data centers / energy / water** → IEA, LBNL/Berkeley Lab, EIA, grid-operator data (PJM/Monitoring Analytics), Data Center Frontier/Dynamics, Data Center Watch, IEEFA, local utility-commission filings.
- **Generative AI / labs / bubble** → S-1s and filings, Artificial Analysis & Epoch AI (capability/compute), The Information, Stratechery, SemiAnalysis, plus the contrarian/financial-skeptic set.
- **Regulation / antitrust / tech right** → court dockets and DOJ/FTC/EC texts, MLex, Politico, Tech Policy Press, primary EO/legislative text.

## Primary & financial sources
- **SEC EDGAR** — 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, S-1/prospectuses, proxies. The ground truth for audited figures; read the S-1 risk factors and the MD&A, not just the headline.
- **Company investor relations** — earnings-call transcripts, shareholder letters, capex guidance, sustainability reports (read critically — see greenwashing caveats).
- **Court records & regulator texts** — PACER/CourtListener dockets; DOJ and FTC complaints/rulings; European Commission decisions; DSA/DMA designations and the **DSA Transparency Database**.
- **Patent & standards** — USPTO, EPO, WIPO; standards bodies for the infrastructure layer.

## Energy, grid & data-center sources
- **IEA** — *Energy and AI* and electricity reports; the anchor for data-center demand trajectories.
- **LBNL / Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory** — US data-center energy-use reports (the most-cited independent baseline).
- **EIA** — US electricity data and forecasts.
- **Grid operators & monitors** — PJM and peers (ISOs/RTOs) for capacity-auction results and interconnection queues; **Monitoring Analytics** (PJM's independent market monitor) for cost-causation analysis; FERC filings for behind-/front-of-meter rulings.
- **IEEFA** (Institute for Energy Economics and Financial Analysis) — critical analysis of utility and energy economics.
- **Data Center Frontier**, **Data Center Dynamics** — industry trade press (track buildout, power deals; read as industry-interested).
- **Data Center Watch** — tracker of community opposition and delayed/canceled projects.
- **Utility-commission dockets** — state PUC filings reveal who is asked to pay (ratepayer vs. data center).
- **SemiAnalysis** — datacenter/power and accelerator deep dives (paid; industry-respected).

## Generative-AI & compute trackers
- **Artificial Analysis** — independent model capability, price, and latency benchmarking.
- **Epoch AI** — compute, scaling, training-cost and trend data (rigorous, citable).
- **Stratechery** (Ben Thompson) — strategy and business-model analysis (sharp; pro-industry priors — read for framework, not verdict).
- **The Information** — premium investigative tech business reporting; strong on deals, finances, lab internals.
- **SemiAnalysis** — chips, HBM, interconnect, cluster economics.
- For the **bubble/skeptic** side: financial-skeptic analysts and short-sellers (e.g. documented depreciation critiques), MIT/Deloitte enterprise-adoption studies, and contrarian finance writers — always traced to the primary study or filing.

## Critical tech media & investigative outlets
- **Rest of World** — Global South tech coverage; lead here for non-US and decolonial angles.
- **404 Media** — tech accountability (ex-Motherboard); strong on AI harms, scraping, platform abuse.
- **Platformer** (Casey Newton) — platform policy and trust-and-safety deep dives.
- **Tech Policy Press** — governance, regulation, platform-power analysis.
- **The Markup / themarkup-style accountability journalism** — algorithmic-audit reporting.
- **Tech Won't Save Us** (Paris Marx) — anti-solutionist critique (polemical; for framing).
- **Logic(s) Magazine** — critical tech essays.
- **Pivot / Hard Fork** — mainstream industry takes; useful for the consensus narrative to test against, with awareness of tech-adjacent bias.

## Think tanks & research
- **AI Now Institute** — power-centered AI governance and accountability.
- **Data & Society** — sociotechnical research.
- **Carnegie Endowment** — tech, AI governance, and geopolitics (serious policy analysis).
- **Public Citizen** — corporate-power and Big-Tech accountability advocacy; tracks AI/political-power issues.
- **EFF** and **Access Now** — digital rights (US and global).
- **Mozilla Foundation** — openness, AI accountability research.
- Awareness flag: identify funders, especially for any tech-funded institute; discount research that mirrors a funder's interest.

## Wire services & financial press
- **Reuters**, **AP**, **AFP** — highest-reliability breaking news; corroborate with 2+ and wait for primary confirmation.
- **Bloomberg** — comprehensive and fast; strong data and energy/AI cost reporting.
- **Financial Times** — quality analysis, less US-centric.
- **The Economist** (neoliberal editorial stance), **Wall Street Journal** (conservative editorial stance) — note priors and read past the editorial line.

## Academic & technical
- **Nature**, **Science** — AI research and tech-policy pieces (peer-reviewed; citable for algorithmic-effect studies).
- **arXiv** — technical preprints (not peer-reviewed; verify claims and check for contamination in benchmarks).
- **SSRN** — law/policy/econ preprints.
- Research groups: **Stanford HAI**, **Oxford Internet Institute**, **Berkman Klein Center**, **MIT** (with awareness of the Media Lab/Epstein-funding legacy). Read institutional priors.

## Geopolitical & China sources

- **MLex**, **EURACTIV**, **Politico Europe** — EU regulatory and Brussels coverage.

## Source-usage guidelines
- **Breaking news:** wires first (Reuters/AP/AFP) → cross-reference 2+ → wait for primary confirmation.
- **Analysis:** specialized outlets (The Information, SemiAnalysis, Stratechery, Platformer) → academic research → ground in primary filings/court/regulator/IEA data.
- **Numbers:** trace every load-bearing figure to a primary source or ≥2 independent ones; state whether it is audited, run-rate, modeled, or undisclosed; flag `CONFLITO DE FONTES` when sources disagree and present both with a quality assessment.
- **Global South:** lead with Rest of World and local-language sources; apply the decolonial lens to source selection itself.
- **Contrarian discipline:** for any major claim, deliberately seek the bear case, the critics, and the failed pilots; note where Exa and Tavily surface different sources — divergence is signal.
