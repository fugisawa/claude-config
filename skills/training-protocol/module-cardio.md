# Module: Cardio / Conditioning

> Medical clearance first if there's any cardiovascular history. Build the base before the intensity.

## Heart-rate zones — anchor on feel (numbering varies)

Two zone systems coexist and **number differently**, so anchor on *feel*, not the number:

| Effort | Feel / talk test | Purpose | 3-zone | 5-zone |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Easy / aerobic base** | full sentences, could go for hours | base, recovery, mitochondria, fat ox | Z1 | Z1–Z2 |
| **Threshold "grey zone"** | comfortably hard, short sentences | sustained-effort fitness — use sparingly | Z2 | Z3 |
| **Hard / VO2** | can't talk | top-end aerobic power | Z3 | Z4–Z5 |

> **"Zone 2 training"** in popular usage = the **easy aerobic base** (≈60–75% HRmax, conversational) — i.e. **Z1** in the 3-zone polarized model, *not* the grey zone. The number is ambiguous; the talk test isn't.

Set thresholds off **LTHR** (lactate-threshold HR, ~a 30-min time-trial avg) when possible; `220−age` is a rough max only.

## The base: easy aerobic (popular "Zone 2")

Most of your aerobic return comes from **easy, conversational volume** — full sentences, never "annoying to talk". Build **duration first** (e.g. 30→60 min). Drifting too hard, into the grey zone, is the #1 mistake. Low recovery cost, so it stacks well on a mobility/yoga base.

## Distribution: polarized 80/20

~**80% easy, ~20% hard**, little in the grey middle. More time in the grey zone ("threshold creep") raises fatigue without the adaptation of either end. Easy days truly easy so hard days are truly hard.

## VO2max intervals (the 20%)

- **Norwegian 4×4:** 4 × 4 min @ ~90–95% HRmax, 3 min easy between. ~1×/week. Strong VO2max stimulus.
- **Short intervals (30/15, 40/20):** repeats of hard/easy; accumulate time near VO2max with less perceived strain.
- Always warm up; never on a fatigued/under-recovered day.

## Threshold work

Tempo / cruise intervals at ~Z2 (LTHR-ish). Useful for sustained-effort events, but **use sparingly** — it's the seductive middle that polarized training warns against.

## Progression & integration

- **Volume before intensity.** Add easy minutes for weeks before adding a second hard session.
- One quality (hard) session/week early; up to two as the base grows; include a **deload** every 3–5 weeks.
- **Integration:** easy Z2 costs little recovery and pairs fine with loaded-mobility days. Interval days need **their own recovery slot** — don't stack them on hard mobility/strength or hot-yoga peaks.

## Metrics & re-test

Track HR at a fixed pace (or pace/power at a fixed HR), RPE, and weekly easy/hard minutes. **Re-test every ~4 weeks:** same route HR drop, or a short time trial. Falling HR at the same pace = the base is building.

## Safety

- Warm up before intensity; cool down.
- Stop for chest pain, dizziness, unusual breathlessness — re-clear medically.
- New to high intensity: earn it with weeks of Z2 base first.
