# Module: Mobility / Flexibility

## Method (the engine of permanent range)

Passive stretch borrows range that's gone in hours; **loading the lengthened position** teaches the nervous system the range is safe and makes it permanent. Loaded mobility is like strength — it needs rest to supercompensate, so **4 loaded sessions + daily anchor beat 14 passive ones**.

**PAILs/RAILs** (the core technique):
1. Settle into end range, hold ~2 min.
2. **PAILs** — *slowly ramp* a contraction into the stretched tissue toward **near-maximal** effort (start ~20%, build over 10–20 s; novices may cap ~60% while learning).
3. **RAILs** — then a *rapid, hard* contraction of the agonist to **actively pull the joint deeper** into the new range (~10–20 s).
4. Sink to the new range. Never sharp pain.

Every position has a side that lengthens and a side that shortens — train both.

## Structure: deficit → arsenal

Diagnose the real limiter (often not the obvious muscle), then assign a tool list. Example shape:

> **Lock A — "butterfly rounds the spine."** Usually stiff T-spine + can't tip the pelvis under adductor tension, not the adductors themselves.
> Arsenal: foam roll + wheel (T-spine), banded frog, Jefferson curl, good morning.

Keep the daily anchor separate from the hard sessions.

## Daily anchor — CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations)

Largest controlled circle of the joint, slow, whole-body tension (irradiation). Not a stretch — active range maintenance + neural mapping of the joint. 3–5 reps each direction, each side, ~5 min, every day including rest days.

## Cueing standard (per exercise)

- **Dose** (sets×time/reps), **equipment**, the **one cue** that matters, the **error to avoid**.
- Label the warning "**Dica / Foco / Atenção**" — not "Evita" (reads wrong in PT-BR).
- A **YouTube-searchable name** (official or in parentheses) so the user can find a tutorial.
- Translations must be *meaningful*, not literal word-for-word from English; research the movement if unclear.

## Principles specific to mobility

- **Load beats passivity** — permanent change needs loaded end-range.
- **Depth before load** — in Jefferson curl / good morning / Cossack, progress is *how deep you control*, not kilos.
- **Warm, always** — lotus/split/bridge never cold (the classic injury).
- **Film every 4 weeks** from the side — the camera shows the week-16 jump against the week-0 baseline.

## Honest timeline + anatomy caveat

Phase it (Pattern → Load → Expression → Continuous) with realistic ranges. State the ceiling honestly: full lotus and flat side-split depend on **hip morphology** (femoral anteversion, acetabular depth) — for some they're unsafe to force, and that's fine. **Knee pain in lotus = STOP, never push.** Goal is deep, safe external rotation, not the final shape at any cost.
